Regex cheatsheet

For grep -E, sed -E, awk, vim's /, and most languages' regex libraries.

Single-character matchers

PatternMatches
aThe literal a
.Any single character (except newline by default)
[abc]One of a, b, c
[a-z]One lowercase letter
[A-Z0-9]One uppercase letter or digit
[^abc]One char NOT in the set

Anchors

PatternMatches
^Start of line
$End of line
\bWord boundary
\BNot a word boundary

Quantifiers

PatternMeans
*Zero or more
+One or more (ERE)
?Zero or one (ERE)
{n}Exactly n
{n,}n or more
{n,m}Between n and m

Grouping / alternation

PatternMeans

In BRE, escape ?, +, {, }, |, (, ) with \ to use them as operators. Easier: just use ERE.

Always

  • Quote your pattern in the shell: '^[A-Z]+$'.
  • Anchor when you mean to: ^pattern, pattern$, or both.
  • Greedy is the default. Use [^X]* for "lazy-ish" in BRE/ERE.
